Linked-Grad Overview

Often, students ask instructors to be a reference for them as they leave school to look for work. In order for an instructor to be a reference, the student must complete a "Student Record Reference Request Consent" form (see sample below). This has worked well for the "traditional" means of being a reference (students giving potential employers the instructor's contact information and then employers contacting by phone, email, etc. to ask questions about the student as a potential employee). Where it fails is in the current world of web-based resumes and endorsements.

On the web, students may choose to set up profiles and/or resumes on various kinds of sites, such as Linked-In, [MORE SAMPLES]. In those contexts, students may want instructors to act as a reference or to "endorse" them for certain skills, abilities, or characteristics. What makes these online references/endorsements unique is that they easily become "perpetual", whereas the typical reference request has an expiry (e.g.: 1 year).

Sample Student Record Reference Request Consent

I, request that write a letter of reference or respond to a reference check on my behalf.

I understand that will have to disclose personal information regarding myself including grades, attendance information and personal characteristics, in order to write a letter of reference or respond to a reference check on my behalf and I consent to this disclosure.

I consent to the disclosure of my above described personal information:

This consent will be effective for a period of after the signature date.

This form constitutes a consent to the disclosure of personal information pursuant to the Freedom of Information and Protection of Privacy Act – section 40 (1) (d). If you have any questions regarding the use of this form, contact General Counsel at [ENTER SCHOOL NAME-LOCATION-PHONE].
